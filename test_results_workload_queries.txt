### Summary of Scope

Installation Support Services (ISS) at Al Dhafra Air Base (ADAB), United Arab Emirates, under the Air Force Contract Augmentation Program (AFCAP) V Task Order, encompass contractor-managed support for the 380th Expeditionary Force Support Squadron (EFSS). The core services include security escorts, customer service at Community Recreation Program (CRP) locations, recreation and fitness operations, lodging logistics and linen support, equipment/appliance maintenance, and water delivery, as detailed in the Performance Work Statement (PWS) dated 4 April 2025. These services operate across a base period (4 May 2026 to 3 May 2027, including 5-day transition) and up to four 1-year option periods, with potential 6-month extensions under FAR 52.217-8. Workload is driven by 32 performance objectives (POs) in the Contractor Performance Objectives and Thresholds Table, emphasizing zero-tolerance thresholds (e.g., no discrepancies in escorts), uptime requirements (e.g., 95% for equipment), and monthly surveillance. Facilities covered span ~286,698 sq ft (Table 1.1), including the Community Activity Center (C.A.C.), fitness centers, dormitories, and water storage. Labor is primarily U.S. Nationals (USNs) for key roles, with Local Nationals/Other Country Nationals (LN/OCNs) for support; total proposed full-time equivalents (FTEs) in the staffing matrix are placeholders (often 0, requiring estimation). MANDATORY requirements (e.g., 24/7 coverage, 1-hour emergency response) ensure mission continuity in a high-security contingency environment, while IMPORTANT ones (e.g., monthly reporting) support oversight. Implicit workloads include 3-shift rotation for 24/7 operations (~5.2 FTEs per position assuming 40-hour weeks) and surge capacity (e.g., 30% for furniture moves). All metrics below are derived from PWS sections, appendices, and CDRLs for basis-of-estimate (BOE) calculations, assuming 2,080 annual hours per FTE.

Workload drivers are grouped by PWS logical sections/categories: General/Administrative (Sections 1.x), On-Site Operations (Section 4.x), Standby/Contingency (Section 6.x), Key Personnel (Section 5.0), and Appendices (F: Recreation/Fitness; G: Lodging; H: Maintenance; I: Water Delivery). Security Escorts and Customer Service are integrated under Section 4.x as core enablers.

### Government-Furnished Equipment (GFE) and Facilities (GFF) Overview

The Performance Work Statement (PWS) for Installation Support Services (ISS) at Al Dhafra Air Base (ADAB) under the Air Force Contract Augmentation Program (AFCAP) outlines limited government support to promote contractor self-sufficiency. Section 3.0 specifies no Government Furnished Property (GFP), with Government Furnished Equipment (GFE) treated as a subset available only through task order modifications and government control. GFF is restricted to facilitative resources without asset liability for the contractor. All GFE and GFF must be used for official contract performance only, returned in serviceable condition (minus normal wear and tear), and subject to Contracting Officer's Representative (COR) inspections and approvals. Improper use triggers a 72-hour remedy period under Section 3.2.1. Contractor-furnished alternatives are implied for non-provided items, as the contract emphasizes contractor accountability for all non-Government resources. No explicit cost savings calculations are detailed in the PWS; however, GFE/GFF reduces contractor procurement needs by providing essential logistics tools and workspaces, aligning with the Task Order's focus on minimal government aid. Delivery dependencies include availability at contract start (4 May 2026, with transition by 9 May 2026), COR joint inventories within 30 days of period of performance (POP) start, and annual reporting via CDRL A016. Risks include delays in COR approvals for access or modifications, potential equipment condition degradation requiring contractor reporting within 24 hours, and base security restrictions limiting access hours.

#### Section 3.0: Government Property, GFF, and Government Furnished Services (GFS)
This section establishes the baseline: zero GFP (no equipment, vehicles, tools, or special test equipment furnished except via modification), with GFF limited to non-accountable facilities for workspace support. GFS is referenced but not detailed beyond equivalence to military/DoD civilian levels for administrative tools.

**1. Equipment Provided by Government (GFE):**  
None under this section, as GFP is explicitly zero. GFE, if introduced via modification, would require separate scheduling, inventory, and Wide Area Workflow (WAWF) reporting. Maintenance responsibility falls to the government, with contractor performing operator-level checks only if directed.

**2. Facilities Provided by Government (GFF):**
