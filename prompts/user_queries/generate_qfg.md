# Questions for the Government (QFG) User Query Prompt

**Purpose**: Generate strategic clarification questions for government during pre-proposal phase to reduce risk, validate assumptions, and shape requirements toward company strengths.

**Target User**: Capture managers drafting RFI responses, proposal managers identifying ambiguities before proposal kickoff, business development identifying opportunities to influence requirements.

**Value Proposition**: Shipley-based QFG methodology transforms ambiguous RFPs into competitive advantages. Well-crafted questions clarify intent, expose gaps, and subtly steer evaluation toward your discriminators. Average RFP has 30-50 legitimate clarification opportunities.

**Shipley Methodology**: Questions for the Government per Shipley Capture Guide - "Ask questions that reduce your risk while increasing competitors' uncertainty."

---

## Response Format Instructions

When the user requests questions for the government, clarification questions, or asks "what should I ask the CO," analyze the knowledge graph and respond using this structure:

│   1. Clarify ambiguous requirements → reduce proposal risk          │
│   2. Expose gaps/conflicts → force government amendments             │
│   3. Shape evaluation criteria → favor your discriminators          │
│   4. Test assumptions → validate capture strategy before bid         │
│   5. Gather intelligence → understand agency priorities/pain points │
│                                                                      │
│ QUESTION CATEGORIES IDENTIFIED:                                      │
│   ├─ Risk Reduction (Ambiguity Clarification): [count] questions    │

│   ├─ Competitive Positioning (Shaping Questions): [count]           │
│   ├─ Cost/Pricing (Budget/IGCE Validation): [count]                 │

│                                                                      │

└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

**Strategic Philosophy**:

- **Ask questions competitors won't**: Expose gaps that force government to clarify/amend
- **Favor your strengths**: Shape evaluation toward your discriminators (without being obvious)
- **Reduce your risk**: Clarify ambiguities that could cause non-compliance
- **Avoid revealing weaknesses**: Never ask questions that expose your capability gaps

### 2. RISK REDUCTION QUESTIONS (Ambiguity Clarification)

**Purpose**: Clarify ambiguous/contradictory requirements that could cause non-compliance or rework.

```
CATEGORY 1: RISK REDUCTION - AMBIGUITY CLARIFICATION ([count] questions)

QUESTION 1:
RFP Reference: [Section L Para X.X, PWS Para Y.Y]
Risk Identified: [Ambiguity/contradiction description]

Current RFP Language:
"[exact quote from RFP showing ambiguity - e.g., 'Section L states 30 pages max, but Section M requires detailed past performance narratives which typically need 40+ pages']"

Why This Creates Risk:
[Specific risk - e.g., "Unclear whether past performance narratives count against 30-page limit. Could result in non-responsive bid if exceeded, or weak scoring if truncated."]

Recommended Question:
"Section L, Paragraph 4.3.1 specifies a 30-page limit for the Technical Volume. Section M, Paragraph 5.2.3 requires detailed narratives for each past performance project. Does the 30-page limit include past performance narratives, or are they submitted as a separate attachment not counted against the page limit?"

Strategic Value:
├─ [PRIMARY]: Clarifies page limit → enables accurate proposal planning
├─ [SECONDARY]: If government allows separate attachment, increases space for win themes
└─ [COMPETITIVE]: Competitors may not catch this, risk exceeding limit

Expected Government Response:
├─ Option 1: "Past performance narratives count toward 30-page limit" → [adjust outline]
├─ Option 2: "Past performance narratives are separate attachment" → [competitive advantage]
└─ Option 3: Amendment issued raising page limit → [reduces compression risk for all]

---

QUESTION 2:
[... repeat structure for all risk reduction questions ...]

---
```

**Common Ambiguity Types**:

- Page limits that don't add up (Section L vs. evaluation factor page needs)
- Conflicting requirements (Section C says X, Section H says Y)
- Undefined terms ("significant experience" - how many years/dollars?)
- Missing submission details (file format, naming convention, portal upload process)

### 3. COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION QUESTIONS (Technical Feasibility)

**Purpose**: Verify technical assumptions that could cause non-compliance or cost overruns.

```
CATEGORY 2: COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION - TECHNICAL FEASIBILITY ([count] questions)

QUESTION 1:
RFP Reference: [PWS Para X.X, Section C CLIN 0001]
Assumption to Validate: [Technical assumption requiring verification]

Current RFP Language:
"[exact quote - e.g., 'Contractor shall provide 24/7/365 helpdesk support with 15-minute response time for Priority 1 issues']"

Why This Needs Clarification:
[Feasibility concern - e.g., "PWS requires 15-minute response but doesn't specify whether this is acknowledgment, diagnosis, or resolution. Impacts staffing levels by 300%."]

Recommended Question:
"PWS Paragraph 3.2.1 requires 15-minute response time for Priority 1 helpdesk issues. Does 'response time' mean: (a) initial acknowledgment of ticket, (b) technician assigned and diagnosing, or (c) issue fully resolved?"

Strategic Value:
├─ [PRIMARY]: Clarifies performance standard → accurate staffing/cost estimate
├─ [SECONDARY]: If "acknowledgment only," reduces required staffing by 60%
└─ [COMPETITIVE]: Competitors may over-staff if they assume "full resolution"

Cost Impact:
├─ Assumption 1 (acknowledgment): [X FTEs] → [$Y total contract cost]
├─ Assumption 2 (diagnosis): [X+5 FTEs] → [$Y+$500K total]
├─ Assumption 3 (resolution): [X+15 FTEs] → [$Y+$2.5M total]
└─ [Validation required before pricing]

Expected Government Response:
├─ Option 1: "Acknowledgment only" → [price competitively]
├─ Option 2: "Full resolution" → [re-evaluate bid/no-bid based on staffing feasibility]
└─ Option 3: Amendment issued clarifying → [all offerors have same understanding]

---

QUESTION 2:
[... repeat structure for all compliance verification questions ...]

---
```

**Common Verification Types**:

- Performance standards (response times, availability, quality metrics)
- Deliverable formats (file types, templates, data schemas)
- Access/security (clearances, facility requirements, SCIF access)
- Government-furnished equipment/info (GFE, GFI, data availability)

### 4. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING QUESTIONS (Shaping Questions)

**Purpose**: Subtly shape evaluation criteria toward your discriminators without revealing your hand.

**CAUTION**: These are advanced questions. Use sparingly and ensure they sound like legitimate clarifications.

```
CATEGORY 3: COMPETITIVE POSITIONING - SHAPING QUESTIONS ([count] questions)

QUESTION 1:
RFP Reference: [Section M Para X.X]
Your Discriminator: [Company strength to emphasize]

Current RFP Language:
"[exact quote - e.g., 'Technical approach will be evaluated on feasibility and innovation']"

Why This Needs "Clarification":
[Legitimate ambiguity that ALSO favors your strength - e.g., "Section M evaluates 'innovation' but doesn't specify whether process innovation (our strength) vs. technology innovation (competitor strength) is weighted higher."]

Recommended Question:
"Section M, Paragraph 5.1.2 evaluates 'innovation' as part of the Technical Approach factor. Does the government prioritize process innovation (e.g., workflow optimization, efficiency improvements) or technology innovation (e.g., new tools, emerging tech adoption)?"

Strategic Value:
├─ [PRIMARY - Legitimate]: Clarifies evaluation criteria → better proposal targeting
├─ [SECONDARY - Shaping]: If government says "both," allows emphasis on YOUR process innovation
├─ [COMPETITIVE]: Plants idea that process innovation matters (competitor focused on tech)
└─ [CAUTION]: Don't make it obvious you're steering toward your strength

Your Discriminator (Internal Context - DO NOT include in question):
[Company has 15 years of proven process optimization experience but limited cutting-edge tech. Want to ensure process innovation is valued equally.]

Expected Government Response:
├─ Option 1: "Both equally valued" → [emphasize process innovation in proposal]
├─ Option 2: "Technology innovation preferred" → [re-evaluate teaming for tech partner]
└─ Option 3: Amendment clarifies criteria → [adjust win themes accordingly]

**RED FLAG CHECK**: Does this question reveal a weakness or bias?
└─ [PASS - question is neutral, could be asked by any offeror]

---

QUESTION 2:
[... repeat structure for all shaping questions ...]

---
```

**Shipley Shaping Principles**:

- Frame as legitimate ambiguity (never "please favor our approach")
- Use neutral language (don't reveal your bias)
- Limit to 3-5 shaping questions max (too many = obvious)
- Ensure question could be asked by ANY offeror (not just you)

**Example Shaping Opportunities**:

- Evaluation weights (clarify subfactor weights to favor your strengths)
- Past performance relevance (ask if commercial experience counts vs. only government)
- Key personnel qualifications (ask if certifications matter vs. only years of experience)
- Small business credit (clarify if small business teaming gets evaluation preference)

### 5. COST/PRICING QUESTIONS (Budget Validation)

**Purpose**: Validate budget assumptions without revealing your pricing strategy.

```
CATEGORY 4: COST/PRICING - BUDGET VALIDATION ([count] questions)

QUESTION 1:
RFP Reference: [Section B CLIN structure, Section J Budget Exhibit]
Pricing Assumption to Validate: [Assumption affecting cost estimate]

Current RFP Language:
"[exact quote - e.g., 'CLIN 0001: Base Year Labor, Firm-Fixed-Price (FFP)']"

Why This Needs Clarification:
[Budget impact - e.g., "Section B shows FFP for labor, but Section C PWS includes variable workload requirements. Unclear if FFP assumes fixed staffing levels or if we must absorb workload spikes."]

Recommended Question:
"Section B CLIN 0001 specifies Firm-Fixed-Price for Base Year Labor. Section C PWS Paragraph 3.1.5 states workload may vary by ±20% based on operational tempo. Is the contractor expected to absorb workload variations within the FFP, or will the government issue modifications for significant workload changes?"

Strategic Value:
├─ [PRIMARY]: Clarifies pricing risk → accurate cost estimate
├─ [SECONDARY]: If government absorbs risk, enables more competitive pricing
└─ [COMPETITIVE]: Competitors may overprice if they assume FFP absorbs all risk

Cost Impact:
├─ Scenario 1 (FFP absorbs risk): [include 20% contingency] → [$X higher price]
├─ Scenario 2 (Mods for >20% changes): [no contingency needed] → [$Y competitive price]
└─ [Pricing strategy depends on government response]

Expected Government Response:
├─ Option 1: "FFP absorbs all risk" → [price accordingly with contingency]
├─ Option 2: "Mods for >20% changes" → [price more competitively]
└─ Option 3: Amendment changes contract type to T&M → [re-price entirely]

---

QUESTION 2:
[... repeat structure for all cost/pricing questions ...]

---
```

**Common Pricing Verification Types**:

- Contract type ambiguities (FFP vs. T&M for specific CLINs)
- Budget exhibits (does exhibit represent ceiling or target?)
- Travel/ODC assumptions (is travel reimbursable or included in FFP?)
- Option year escalation (can we include annual escalation or is pricing fixed for all years?)

### 6. EVALUATION CRITERIA QUESTIONS (Scoring Clarification)

**Purpose**: Understand how government will score proposals to target proposal content appropriately.

```
CATEGORY 5: EVALUATION CRITERIA - SCORING CLARIFICATION ([count] questions)

QUESTION 1:
RFP Reference: [Section M Para X.X]
Scoring Ambiguity: [Unclear evaluation methodology]

Current RFP Language:
"[exact quote - e.g., 'Past Performance will be assessed based on relevance, recency, and quality']"

Why This Needs Clarification:
[Scoring impact - e.g., "Section M states past performance assessed on 'relevance' but doesn't define. Unclear if commercial contracts count, if subcontractor experience counts, or if only prime federal contracts matter."]

Recommended Question:
"Section M, Paragraph 5.3 evaluates Past Performance based on 'relevance.' Does the government consider the following types of experience relevant: (a) commercial contracts with similar scope, (b) subcontractor experience under federal contracts, (c) prime contracts with different agencies? Additionally, is experience from the past 3 years weighted more heavily than older experience?"

Strategic Value:
├─ [PRIMARY]: Clarifies what to include in past performance volume
├─ [SECONDARY]: If commercial counts, expands our reference pool significantly
└─ [COMPETITIVE]: Competitors with only federal experience may be limited if commercial counts

Proposal Impact:
├─ If commercial counts: [include 5 additional projects] → [stronger past performance]
├─ If only federal counts: [limited to 3 projects] → [need teaming partner for more]
└─ If subcontractor counts: [leverage partner's federal contracts] → [strengthens team]

Expected Government Response:
├─ Option 1: "Federal prime only" → [assess teaming needs]
├─ Option 2: "Commercial counts if similar scope" → [include commercial projects]
└─ Option 3: Amendment clarifies weighting → [prioritize proposal content accordingly]

---

QUESTION 2:
[... repeat structure for all evaluation questions ...]

---
```

**Common Evaluation Clarifications**:

- Adjectival ratings (what's difference between Excellent vs. Good?)
- Subfactor weights (which subfactors matter most within major factor?)
- Trade-off approach (technical vs. price - how will LPTA vs. best-value work?)
- Evaluation team composition (will technical SMEs or contracting officers evaluate technical?)

### 7. QFG SUBMISSION FORMAT

**Purpose**: Final formatted questions ready to submit to government.

```
QUESTIONS FOR THE GOVERNMENT - FINAL SUBMISSION
Submitted by: [Company Name]
Submission Date: [Date]
POC: [Name, email, phone]

---

QUESTION 1:
RFP Section: [Section L, Paragraph 4.3.1]
Question: [Full question text as written above]

---

QUESTION 2:
RFP Section: [Section M, Paragraph 5.2.3]
Question: [Full question text]

---

[Continue for all questions - typically 15-25 max]

---

SUBMISSION NOTES:
├─ Questions submitted via: [email address or portal per Section A]
├─ Submission deadline: [date/time]
├─ Government response expected: [date - typically 7-14 days after deadline]
└─ Follow-up: [monitor for amendments incorporating clarifications]
```

**Submission Best Practices**:

- Group related questions by RFP section (easier for CO to answer)
- Number questions sequentially (1, 2, 3...)
- Cite exact RFP paragraph references (Section L Para 4.3.1, not "Section L")
- Keep questions neutral/professional (avoid advocacy language)
- Limit to 25 questions max (COs ignore if you submit 100+ questions)

## Important Reminders

### What This Prompt DOES:

✅ Generate 15-25 strategic clarification questions targeting ambiguities  
✅ Identify contradictions/gaps requiring government amendment  
✅ Shape evaluation criteria toward your discriminators (subtly)  
✅ Validate cost/pricing assumptions before proposal pricing  
✅ Reduce proposal risk by clarifying technical feasibility  
✅ Provide submission-ready question format for CO

### What This Prompt DOES NOT DO:

❌ Guarantee government will answer (some COs ignore QFG submissions)  
❌ Replace capture strategy development (questions inform strategy, don't create it)  
❌ Reveal your pricing strategy (never ask "what's the budget?")  
❌ Expose your weaknesses (never ask questions that show capability gaps)  
❌ Guarantee amendments (government may answer "see RFP as written")

**Shipley Philosophy**: "Ask questions that reduce YOUR risk while increasing COMPETITORS' uncertainty. Never ask questions that reveal your weaknesses."

## Quality Indicators

**High-Quality Response Includes**:

- Exact RFP paragraph citations for every question
- Strategic value explanation (why this question matters)
- Multiple expected government responses with decision trees
- Cost/schedule impact analysis for pricing questions
- Red flag check for shaping questions (neutrality verification)
- Submission-ready format with POC info

**Low-Quality Response Includes**:

- Generic questions applicable to any RFP (not tailored to THIS RFP)
- No strategic rationale (doesn't explain why question matters)
- Obvious advocacy questions ("Will you give us credit for X?")
- Too many questions (50+ overwhelms CO, gets ignored)
- No RFP citations (CO can't find what you're asking about)
- Questions that reveal your capability gaps

## Shipley Capture Guide Integration

**Key Principles** (Shipley Capture Guide, p. 65-75):

1. **Questions as Intelligence**: QFG responses reveal government priorities, budget, pain points
2. **Risk Reduction**: Clarify ambiguities BEFORE proposal kickoff to prevent rework
3. **Competitive Shaping**: Subtly steer evaluation toward your strengths (without being obvious)
4. **Avoid Revealing Weaknesses**: Never ask questions that expose capability gaps
5. **Monitor for Amendments**: Government answers often result in RFP amendments (competitive advantage if you prompted the clarification)

**Question Types** (Shipley Framework):

- **Clarification**: Resolve ambiguities/contradictions (risk reduction)
- **Validation**: Test technical/pricing assumptions (feasibility)
- **Shaping**: Influence evaluation criteria (competitive positioning)
- **Intelligence**: Understand government priorities/budget (capture strategy)

**QFG Best Practices**:

- Submit 15-25 questions (enough to clarify, not so many CO ignores)
- Front-load high-priority questions (COs may answer first 10 and stop)
- Track government responses for all offerors (monitor FBO/SAM.gov for amendments)
- Use QFG answers to update capture strategy and proposal outline
