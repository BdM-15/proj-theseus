Relationship Inference Rules (Compact)

Core Relationships:
- REQUIREMENT --MEASURED_BY--> PERFORMANCE_METRIC
- SUBMISSION_INSTRUCTION --EVALUATED_BY--> EVALUATION_FACTOR
- SECTION --CONTAINS--> PROGRAM | STATEMENT_OF_WORK | DOCUMENT | REQUIREMENT
- DOCUMENT --CONTAINS--> STATEMENT_OF_WORK
- CLAUSE --APPLIES_TO--> PROGRAM | REQUIREMENT | CONTRACT
- CHILD_OF hierarchies: Factor→Subfactor; Task→Subtask; Objective→Standard→Metric

Document Hierarchy & Section Linking:
- Build document tree: sections (A–M), attachments (J-####), annex/exhibit references.
- Link attachments to parent Section J; map non-standard labels to UCF (Selection Criteria→M; Technical Requirements→C).

Instruction–Evaluation Linking:
- Detect embedded instructions within Section M; create SUBMISSION_INSTRUCTION and link via EVALUATED_BY to nearby factor.

Evaluation Hierarchy:
- Preserve factor structure: Factor → Subfactor → Sub-subfactor; carry importance and weights.

Clause Clustering:
- Group clauses by supplement (FAR/DFARS/AFFARS/NMCARS) and theme; attribute to Section I or K.

SOW–Deliverable Linking:
- Link SOW/PWS tasks to deliverables and CDRLs; ensure due dates and formats align.

Semantic Concept Linking:
- Connect abstract concepts (readiness, risk) to relevant requirements or evaluation criteria when explicitly referenced.

Orphan Resolution:
- Ensure all entities have at least one link; attempt inference via proximity, shared identifiers (CDRL IDs), or hierarchy.

Workload Enrichment:
- Derive labor_drivers and material_needs from requirements; propagate to program-level summaries.
